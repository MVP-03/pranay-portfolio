# Problem

Mental health conversations are still stigmatized, and most content on the
topic is either clinical or performatively vulnerable. There wasn't a
straightforward, honest place for people to just talk about well-being
without either extreme.

## Approach

Co-founded "You (me) Us" as a space for evidence-based writing on
well-being, paired with a genuinely empathetic, plain-spoken voice - no
jargon, no forced positivity.

## Outcome

Built a recognizable voice for the blog and a small but real audience
around open dialogue on mental health, with recognition for clear
communication and consistent, honest writing.
