# Problem

Class imbalance quietly breaks real-world ML: a fraud model trained on
data that's 577:1 majority-to-minority can hit 99%+ accuracy by simply
never flagging fraud, and standard oversampling methods (SMOTE and its
variants) generate synthetic samples that never leave the convex hull of
existing data - no genuinely new signal.

## Approach

Led a 4-person team (final-year capstone) building and benchmarking a
hybrid pipeline across three real datasets - Credit Card Fraud (577:1
imbalance), Pima Diabetes, and Breast Cancer Wisconsin. Surveyed classical
methods (SMOTE, Borderline-SMOTE, ADASYN, SVM-SMOTE) and four GAN variants
(cGAN, CTGAN, WGAN-GP, InfoGAN) before landing on the final architecture:
GAN-SMOTE augmented with a Tree-Based Verification layer, where a Random
Forest/XGBoost model audits every synthetic sample and rejects the ones
that land in statistically inconsistent decision regions.

## Outcome

On Breast Cancer, the pipeline reached a perfect PR-AUC of 1.0 and an
F1-score of 0.9841. On the extreme-imbalance Credit Card Fraud dataset, it
hit an F1 of 0.6742 and MCC of 0.6833 - nearly double the MCC of GAN-SMOTE
without verification (0.3527) - by filtering out synthetic samples the
tree auditor flagged as implausible. Written up as a full project report
at GITAM (Deemed to be University).

Tools: Python, PyTorch, TensorFlow, XGBoost, scikit-learn, imbalanced-learn.
